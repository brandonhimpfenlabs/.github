# Brandon Himpfen Labs

Brandon Himpfen Labs is a collection of experimental open source software projects focused on scientific computing, simulation systems, data infrastructure, and applied algorithms.

The purpose of Labs is to explore computational approaches to real-world problems through practical experimentation. Projects typically begin as small prototypes that investigate how systems behave, how data can be processed more efficiently, or how computational models can be structured in a clear and reproducible way.

Many Labs projects are written in **Julia**, a programming language designed for high-performance numerical and scientific computing. Julia enables the development of systems that remain readable while also supporting efficient data processing and simulation workloads.

Learn more at https://www.brandonhimpfen.com/labs/

## Areas of Exploration

Projects developed through Labs generally fall into several categories:

- **Scientific Computing:** Tools and frameworks designed for numerical analysis, modeling, and research workflows.
- **Simulation Systems:** Computational models that explore how complex systems behave under different conditions.
- **Data Infrastructure:** Systems for ingesting, transforming, and structuring datasets used in research and analytical environments.
- **Applied Algorithms:** Experimental implementations of algorithms used for analysis, optimization, and modeling.
